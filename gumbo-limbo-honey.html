Gumbo Limbo trees - also known as “tourist trees” - (since their peeling reddish bark is supposed to resemble sunburned tourists) - are found all over the Florida Keys, on roadsides, gardens, parks and hardwood hammocks alike, and are easily recognizable by visitors as an iconic native of the Keys.

Gumbo Limbo Honey

Gumbo Limbo is a delicious honey.

This nectar produces a light clear honey which is delicious, having a mild sweet flavor distinctive to the species, and like all our honeys, completely uncontaminated by mainland agricultural and industrial chemicals and pollution..

Gumbo Limbos lose their leaves in late March and April, often leaving the tree completely bare for a month or so, but like many other trees native to the Florida Keys, Gumbo Limbos have a period of bloom in April and May, often just before their new leaves start to grow, and the rainy season starts.

KEEZ BEEZ maintains several bee hives in the South Homestead area for collection of nectar from the Mamey fruit tree. These colonies, offspring of our main colonies in the Florida Keys, are installed permanently in quiet locations with private growers who have similar philosophies to our own, and do not use pesticides or other dangerous chemicals on their crops.
